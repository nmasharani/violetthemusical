The show will run Thursday, November 14th at 7PM; Friday, November 15th at 5PM; Saturday, November 16th at Noon and 5PM; Sunday, November 17th at Noon and 5PM. Get your tickets now!

This fall, At The Fountain Theatricals will bring Stanford audiences a production of the Lucille Lortel and Drama Critics' Circle award-winning musical Violet thanks to the generosity of the ReDesigning Theater and Arts Institute's Spark Grants. This piece, with music by Jeanine Tesori and libretto by Brian Crawley, tells the story of a woman who travels cross-country to seek treatment for a wound inflicted in her childhood. Originally produced in 1997 and set in the 1964 South, Violet is meant to be staged as though the characters are on a bus. For At the Fountain's November production, the characters will actually be in that authentic environment, along with the audience, such that the audience can understand Violet's journey first-hand. Instead of traveling cross-country, the bus will stop at campus locations where certain scenes will be performed off the bus. This unique theatrical experience, a travel musical that travels, is sure to be one that Stanford audiences won't want to miss.

Sammi Cannold '16 will direct, Makulumy Alexander-Hills '16 will music direct and Weston Gaylord '15 will vocal direct, Brandon Powell '14 and Kelly Gregg '15 will produce, and Emily Ashton '16 will stage manage. ATF wishes to thank the ReDesigning Theatre Seed Grant and Stanford Arts Institute for their generosity in making this production a possibility. We hope you'll join us on board!
